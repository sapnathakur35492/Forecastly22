Estimation Approach

Top-Down Meets Bottom-Up

We don't rely on a single estimation method. Every market size figure is triangulated using two independent approaches — and only published when both converge within an acceptable variance range.

APPROACH 1

🔻 Top-Down Approach

Starts from the macro level and narrows down. We begin with the total industry output or global market value from established databases, then apply filters to isolate the specific market segment.

How it works:

1. Begin with total industry revenue from government statistics, trade associations, and international bodies (World Bank, IMF, UN Comtrade)

2. Apply segmentation ratios derived from industry reports to isolate the target market

3. Use regional GDP contribution, industry concentration, and trade data to break down by geography

4. Cross-reference with company financial disclosures, annual reports, and investor presentations for revenue benchmarks

Best for: Mature markets with established industry-level data. Provides the overall market ceiling and validates bottom-up estimates.

APPROACH 2

🔺 Bottom-Up Approach

Starts from individual data points and builds upward. We aggregate company-level revenues, unit shipments, pricing data, and capacity utilization to construct the market from its components.

How it works:

1. Identify all major and mid-tier players in the market using competitive intelligence databases

2. Extract segment-specific revenue from annual reports, SEC filings, investor calls, and press releases

3. Estimate private company revenues using employee count proxies, funding data, and market share intelligence

4. Aggregate across all players, add tail-end estimates for smaller firms, and validate against import/export data

Best for: Emerging and fragmented markets. Provides granular accuracy and validates the top-down ceiling.

🎯 Triangulation & Convergence

Both approaches are run independently. If estimates converge within a 5-8% variance, the average is taken as the final figure. If variance exceeds this threshold, our analysts investigate the gap — checking for missing players, data anomalies, or structural market shifts — until the numbers align.

Forecasting

How We Build 10-Year Forecasts

Our forecasts aren't simple trend extrapolations. They're built on a multi-factor model that accounts for industry dynamics, macroeconomic conditions, and market-specific drivers.

📈 Growth Drivers Analyzed

Technology adoption curves, regulatory tailwinds/headwinds, consumer demand shifts, investment inflows, supply chain capacity, competitive intensity, and macroeconomic indicators (GDP growth, inflation, FX rates).

🎯 CAGR Calculation

Compound Annual Growth Rate is calculated from the base year to the forecast end year. Year-on-year growth rates may vary — early years often show higher growth in emerging markets, while mature markets show steady, linear progression.

📊 Country-Level Forecasting

Country-level estimates are not simply global figures divided by GDP ratio. Each country's forecast considers its unique regulatory environment, infrastructure maturity, competitive landscape, import/export dynamics, and domestic demand patterns. For example, India's EV market forecast accounts for the FAME subsidy scheme and charging infrastructure gaps, while Germany's reflects EU emissions mandates and automotive industry restructuring.
